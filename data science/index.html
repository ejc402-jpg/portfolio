BWF Match Analysis & Tournament Prediction Software - September 2nd, 2025

Badminton is a widely popular racket sport around the globe, both recreationally and competitively. However, due to the multitude of factors such as arena drift, player head-to-heads, strategy, and other game dynamics, professional play is often unpredictable to the standard viewer.

Particularly with the 2025 BWF World Championships kicking off as I'm writing this, many online badminton enthusiasts are making their bold predictions for who will ultimately lift the trophies and become world champions for their country. However, can looking at pure empirical statistics be a viable method for reliably predicting tournament outcomes? Or are there factors off the court that affect gameplay, but cannot be properly represented through data?

This project aims to cultivate a more meticulous method of analyzing professional badminton tournamanets, understanding what most winners have in common, and be able to apply these concepts to their own matches or other competitive undertakings.

We can also use these findings to determine which factors ultimately influence match outcomes the most and utilize certain machine learning techniques to craft some (hopefully accurate) power rankings 2025 BWF World Championships. We won't necessarily be able to predict match outcomes based on the actual tournament draw (which could be a potentially bigger project on its own), but we should be able to see which players could be a true threat at the World Championships this year, based on current yearly stats and previous World Championship outcomes.
